Festival Etiquette Without the Guesswork

Simple habits for visiting, giving gifts and joining family celebrations with warmth and consideration.

2026-06-05·6 min read

Table of contents

  1. Ask about the household plan
  2. Keep gifts simple and shareable
  3. Join in with attention

Ask about the household plan

Festival customs vary between families and regions. Ask what time to arrive, whether the meal is casual and if you can bring anything. This is more useful than trying to guess a universal rule.

节日习惯会因家庭和地区而不同。提前询问到达时间、聚餐是否随意，以及是否需要带东西，比猜测一条“通用规则”更实用。

Busy travel periods can affect plans, so confirm close to the day and share delays early. Reliability is itself a form of courtesy.

节日期间出行繁忙，安排可能变化，因此临近日期最好再次确认；如有延误，也要尽早告知。守信本身就是一种礼貌。

Keep gifts simple and shareable

Fruit, tea or a specialty from your home region can be easy to share. Choose tidy packaging, keep the value comfortable for the relationship and mention any dietary ingredients.

水果、茶叶或家乡特产都便于分享。包装整洁即可，价值应符合彼此关系，也别忘了说明可能涉及的食材。

If a host initially declines, a warm “一点心意” (yìdiǎn xīnyì — a small token) explains your intention. Avoid turning the exchange into a contest of insistence.

如果主人起初推辞，可以温和地说“一点心意”，说明自己的用意。无需把礼物往来变成反复坚持的拉锯。

Join in with attention

Follow the host when seating and serving are unfamiliar. Offer help once, accept the answer, and notice practical ways to contribute such as carrying dishes or keeping children company.

如果不熟悉座位或上菜安排，跟随主人即可。主动提出一次帮忙并尊重回答，也可以留意端菜、陪伴孩子等实际可做的小事。

A message the next day thanking the host for one specific moment feels genuine and completes the visit gracefully.

第二天发一条消息，具体感谢聚会中的某个片刻，会显得真诚，也为这次拜访画上圆满句号。
